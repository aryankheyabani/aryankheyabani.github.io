Research Associate

Sabanci University, Istanbul, Turkey

I began my Ph.D. at Sabancı University in 2020 by joining the Computational Mechanics & Engineering Optimization (CMEO) research group led by Associate Professor Dr. Adnan Kefal. During the PhD, I developed a novel computational framework (PHFGMC-IGA-RZT) that integrates high-fidelity microscale modeling with advanced macroscopic analysis techniques. My research combined the Parametric High Fidelity Generalized Method of Cells (PHFGMC), Refined Zigzag Theory (RZT), and Isogeometric Analysis (IGA) to enable accurate simulation of damage progression in layered and sandwich composite materials with complex geometries. The core innovation lies in formulating a multiscale modeling technique that effectively bridges microscale physics with structural-level behavior, resulting in simulations that are both efficient and precise.

This work led to several first-author journal publications and fostered valuable research collaborations. Building on this foundation, I later enhanced the framework with a higher-order RZT formulation and incorporated Continuum Damage Mechanics (CDM) for macro-level progressive damage modeling, marking a significant advancement that has been rigorously validated through numerical and experimental studies.

Throughout my Ph.D., I consistently demonstrated research independence by setting clear objectives, breaking down complex projects into actionable tasks, and advancing beyond established methodologies. A semester at TU Darmstadt further strengthened my autonomy, where I integrated into a new research environment under the supervision of Prof. Dr.-Ing. Dominik Schillinger.

Alongside my research, I actively contributed to teaching and mentoring. I served as a teaching assistant for undergraduate courses in mechanics and computational modeling and supported junior students in their research and programming projects. I am passionate about making technical concepts accessible and engaging, and my clear, structured explanations have been met with consistently positive feedback.

Projects

This section presents the scientific and software development projects I contributed to during my Ph.D. and post-Ph.D. period. The projects are organized in chronological order to highlight the progression of my work and to ensure clarity and ease of understanding.

Numerical Modelling and Experimental Verification on the Effect of Fibre and Resin Hybridization in 3D Orthogonal Woven Polymeric Composite Materials - Researcher

I began my PhD journey by joining this project as a researcher. The objective of the project was to perform experimental and numerical investigations on 3D woven composite materials, focusing on their structural performance. My primary responsibilities included conducting advanced simulations and numerical modeling, and comparing the computational outcomes with experimental data for validation.

Alongside my core project duties, I independently developed a multiscale simulation technique that integrates the Parametric High-Fidelity Generalized Method of Cells (PHFGMC) at the microscale with Refined Zigzag Theory (RZT) defined within an Isogeometric Analysis (IGA) framework. This approach, referred to as PHFGMC-IGA-RZT, was first applied to analyze thick composite laminates and sandwich structures in the linear elastic regime. The results were validated and published in a peer-reviewed journal. I later extended this method to study and compare the mechanical behavior of unidirectional vs. 3D woven sandwich composites, which also resulted in a journal publication.

To improve the modeling capabilities, I formulated a higher-order RZT model within the IGA framework. Both numerical simulations and experimental tests were conducted to assess the accuracy of the linear and higher-order multiscale techniques, and the validated results were disseminated in several journal papers. The following figure shows the algorithm along with the experimental setup and stress-strain curves demonstrating the proposed formulations’ superiority.

Figure 1: Linear framework algorithm, RUC (representative unit cell), experimental setup and sandwich responses

From a software perspective, I conducted simulations using ANSYS and ABAQUS, gaining hands-on experience in nonlinear and linear analysis of composite and metallic materials. Additionally, I developed an in-house MATLAB code that implements the PHFGMC, RZT, and IGA frameworks. These implementations were later integrated into a unified codebase capable of performing complete multiscale analysis of composite laminates.

Development and Experimental Application of Novel Inverse Analysis Methods for Crack Propagation Monitoring and Structural Health Monitoring of Composite Structures - Researcher

In this project, I was responsible for overseeing the experimental phase, including the manufacturing, mechanical testing, and reporting of composite specimens. I also conducted numerical simulations to verify and evaluate the experimental results.

Beyond the core project tasks, I enhanced the previously developed PHFGMC-IGA-RZT multiscale analysis technique to enable progressive damage modeling of composite laminates, sandwich structures, and stiffened plates. This included extending the method to capture nonlinear behavior and failure mechanisms across different material systems and structural configurations.

These developments marked a key milestone in my PhD research, which aimed to establish a comprehensive multiscale framework for simulating the damage and failure of advanced composite materials. As part of this effort, I also addressed challenges related to mesh dependency in failure analysis, improving the reliability of simulation results under various loading conditions. The outcomes of these studies are published in journal papers and my Ph.D. thesis. Figure 2 presents representative results of the enhanced damage modeling technique.

To conduct theoretical studies, I developed and implemented numerical models using ANSYS Workbench and Mechanical APDL. Additionally, I extended the in-house MATLAB PHFGMC-IGA-RZT codebase to incorporate nonlinear analysis capabilities within the multiscale modeling framework. To improve computational performance, I applied vectorization techniques in MATLAB and optimized data structures to reduce execution time. As a result of developing this comprehensive codebase, I gained hands-on experience with the full software development lifecycle, including algorithm design, modular implementation, code optimization, debugging, unit testing, and validation against experimental results.
